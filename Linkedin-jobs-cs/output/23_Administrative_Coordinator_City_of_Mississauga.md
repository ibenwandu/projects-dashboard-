Job Summary

The Administrative Coordinator provides administrative and clerical support to the Development and Design Division. This role ensures timely and accurate coordination of meetings, documents, events, workflows, and division-wide administrative processes, including financial tasks. The position also provides backup support to various business units, manages logistics, provides communication and maintains divisional and team scheduling.

The successful candidate will have strong organizational and communication skills and will play a key role in facilitating efficient service delivery to internal staff, external developers, and the public. Using advanced technological skills and strong organizational abilities, the successful candidate will aid in the efficient day-to-day operation of the division.

Duties and Responsibilities

Reporting to the Director of Development and Design, the successful candidate will:


Assist with the day-to-day administrative requirements for a range of ongoing activities associated with the effective operation of the Development and Design Division
Complete financial tasks for the division as it relates to invoices, fees, securities and deposits for a wide range of matters including development applications
Assist the Urban Design and Development review teams in a wide range of technical aspects such as producing powerpoint presentations and word documents
Support the submission and processing of development related fees and charges
Lead with the planning and organization of divisional related events
Ensure the organization and scheduling of divisional team meetings
Format, organize and file a variety of correspondence, corporate reports, emails, spreadsheets, memorandums and other documents.
Assist with the creation and maintenance of the digital file system, including SharePoint sites
Arrange and schedule meetings, create agendas and set up conference calls, while being responsible for accurate meeting reporting and minutes
Accurately inputting time and labour adhering to strict timelines
Assist with the administrative review of corporate reports
General mailbox email monitoring, ordering supplies, collecting, opening and distributing mail
Answer phones, directing calls and responding to general inquiries and requests for information over the phone, via email and in person
Other duties as assigned


Skills And Qualifications


Post-secondary education in office administration or related field. Minimum 3 years progressive experience and responsibility in an administrative role or equivalent combination of education and experience. Municipal and development planning experience is considered an asset
Possession of excellent computer technology skills in Microsoft Office and Adobe Creative Suite is an asset
Excellent administrative, planning, research, organizational, multi-tasking and time management skills are mandatory
Experience coordinating meetings and electronic appointment scheduling is required
Aptitude for accuracy and attention to details is essential
Excellent verbal and written communication skills, including proof-reading and editing skills. Proficiency in English grammar and spelling are required
Experience with SAP, Infor, MAX and SharePoint is an asset
Demonstrated excellent communication and interpersonal skills to communicate effectively with all levels of management, staff and the general public
