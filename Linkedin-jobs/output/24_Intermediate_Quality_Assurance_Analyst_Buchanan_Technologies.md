Work Model: Hybrid work environment with a minimum of two days onsite per week, with the remaining days remote.

Overview

The Intermediate Quality Assurance (QA) Analyst is responsible for ensuring the quality, reliability, security, and performance of software applications through structured testing and defect management practices. This role works closely with developers, business analysts, and project stakeholders to validate system functionality, ensure proper data access, and support the delivery of high-quality software solutions.

The ideal candidate has strong analytical skills, attention to detail, and experience executing both manual and automated testing across the software development lifecycle.




Key Responsibilities

Develop and execute test plans, test cases, and test scripts based on project requirements and technical specifications.
Perform functional, regression, integration, security, and performance testing to validate application behavior and system performance.
Identify, document, and track software defects, issues, and risks using defect tracking tools.
Collaborate with developers, business analysts, and project stakeholders to understand requirements and ensure comprehensive test coverage.
Reproduce reported issues and verify fixes to confirm defect resolution.
Maintain testing documentation including test plans, test cases, and test data.
Participate in test environment setup, including configuration and deployment of software components.
Contribute to continuous improvement of QA processes, methodologies, and testing tools.
Support the creation and maintenance of automated test scripts and frameworks.
Stay current with emerging technologies, testing tools, and best practices in software quality assurance.




Required Qualifications

University degree, college diploma, or equivalent experience in Computer Science, Engineering, or a related field.
Minimum 3 years of experience in software quality assurance or related SDLC activities.
Demonstrated experience in test planning, test execution, and defect management.
Strong understanding of software testing methodologies, tools, and best practices.
Experience with manual and automated testing tools such as Selenium or similar platforms.
Ability to write and maintain automated test scripts.
Familiarity with test management and defect tracking tools such as Azure DevOps Test Plans.
Experience working with version control systems such as Git.
Familiarity with continuous integration tools such as Jenkins or similar solutions.
Strong analytical, documentation, and communication skills.
Ability to work collaboratively in a team-oriented environment and manage multiple priorities.




Mandatory Certification

Candidates must hold at least one of the following certifications:

Certified Software Testing Engineer (CSTE)
Certified Associate in Software Testing (CAST)
Certified Tester Foundation Level (CTFL)
Certified Software Quality Analyst (CSQA)




Preferred Experience

Experience working in Agile software development environments with user stories and acceptance criteria.
Experience with Azure DevOps or similar test management platforms.
Experience testing Salesforce platforms or AI-based applications.
