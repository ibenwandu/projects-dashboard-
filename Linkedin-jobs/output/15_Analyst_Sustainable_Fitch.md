## Full job description

Sustainable Fitch is currently seeking an Analyst based out of our Toronto office.

Powered by the human insight that has differentiated Fitch for over 100 years, Sustainable Fitch combines experience and heritage with the best ESG information available in the market today. Our suite of solutions provides a much-anticipated, single source for the ESG focused financial community with tools, data, research and insights under one holistic, modular, user-friendly ESG investing umbrella. Sustainable Fitch is a Fitch Solutions Company.

Working at Sustainable Fitch provides the opportunity to engage with sustainability-focused financial analysis within a globally recognized ratings organization. You'll be part of a team dedicated to assessing the environmental, social, and governance (ESG) impacts of investment decisions, shaping the future of sustainable finance. This role allows you to drive positive change by integrating ESG considerations into credit ratings and research.




About The Team

Part of Fitch Group, Sustainable Fitch is focused on research and analysis of Sustainability themes for companies and their debt instruments across the globe. The main products offered by Sustainable Fitch include Ratings, Scores, Second Party Opinions, European Green Bond External Reviews and Research, with the support of Product Development and Resource teams. It has offices in Barcelona, Hong Kong, London, New York, Singapore and Toronto.




How You’ll Make An Impact

Join a growing and strategically important business division of Fitch Group that has a dynamic team of seasoned and passionate regional and global sustainability specialists. Produce sustainability assessments on entities and debt instruments, contribute to our success.
Ongoing training with access to a wide range of learning and training programs and certificates where your sustainability, financial, IT and soft skills will be further developed. We have designed and curated a five-year career plan.
Great benefits package including medical insurance, annual volunteer working days, non-contributory pension, parental leave, four weeks per year allowance to work from abroad, hybrid working arrangements with three days in the office, bonus and a competitive salary.




You May Be a Good Fit If

Understand and apply Sustainable Fitch’s analytical methodologies and become familiar with the company’s approach to assessing the sustainability impact associated with a broad range of business activities.
Carry out and deliver high-quality, timely, focused written analysis on a suite of products related to the sustainability characteristics and performance of entities and debt issuances. Output should be supported by well-construed arguments, backed by verified factual data.
Keep up to date with sustainability trends globally, both regulatory and sector specific.
Interact with colleagues globally to leverage knowledge, gain international experience and establish good working relationships.

What You Need To Have

Degree in a discipline with a technical background related to any environmental science, social risk or governance-themed disciplines, or economics/ business/ finance.
Minimum two to three years’ experience in sustainability and knowledge/ application of current sustainability market themes and regulation, as well as international taxonomies for sustainable activities.
Basic financial literacy. Capable of working with data.
Have strong analytical and critical thinking skills and good attention to detail and comfortable working with and drawing conclusions from data. Attention to detail.
Strong team player with excellent inter-personal skills.
Self-starter that shows initiative and know how to manage and prioritize his/her time.
Language skills. In addition to English, fluency in other languages is beneficial.




What Would Make You Stand Out

Passion for sustainability and related topics, and thorough knowledge of international taxonomies.
Financial flair and a keen interest in credit, fixed income and industry analysis with certain understanding of financial statements.
Microsoft Office skills, including Excel and Word, and ability to work with proprietary models and internal analytical tools.
