## Full job description

Connor, Clark & Lunn Infrastructure

Toronto, ON

Interested in joining one of Canada’s top performing and fastest growing infrastructure investment teams? Connor, Clark & Lunn Infrastructure (“CC&L Infrastructure”) is seeking an Analyst to fill an existing role in our Asset Management Team. As a member of a collaborative, highly-motivated and performance-driven team, you will own the automation of reporting and monitoring workflows across our renewable energy portfolio, while developing the aptitude to enable broader AI adoption across the organization. You will work closely with the Director of Asset Management to build reliable, parameter-driven data pipelines that connect operational data to portfolio-level views that enable valuable decision making. This is an opportunity to work at the intersection of infrastructure investing and modern data automation. Our offices currently operate on a hybrid model with a combination of in office and remote working days throughout the week.

Responsibilities


Build and maintain automated data collection pipelines for asset (Solar, Wind, Hydro) monitoring and reporting, including data ingestion, validation, and output generation;
Build and maintain financial and operating models in Microsoft Excel;
Automate monthly workflows by developing integrations between external data sources with internal work-product;
Develop and maintain integrations to pull data from a variety of sources (API, SFTP, external databases);
Implement validation layers with variance alerts and exception logging to ensure data integrity;
Create and maintain site configuration registries so that adding new assets requires config updates only, not code changes;
Contribute to AI enablement initiatives, including prototyping LLM-powered workflows; and
Document data lineage, create runbooks, and support knowledge transfer to ensure operational continuity.


Qualifications


Undergraduate degree in a quantitative field (engineering, computer science, finance, mathematics, or related);
1-3 years of experience working with financial models (e.g. MS Excel, Power BI);
1-3 years of experience working with data processing (e.g. pandas, openpyxl, pySpark, SQL);
Demonstrated ability to wrangle heterogeneous data from multiple sources;
Experience with Microsoft Fabric or similar data platforms is an asset;
Hands-on experience using LLMs and AI tools (ChatGPT, GitHub Copilot, Claude) for practical work tasks;
Strong problem-solving mindset with attention to detail and data quality;
Excellent communication skills and ability to explain technical work to non-technical stakeholders; and
Interest in infrastructure and energy markets; prior exposure is an asset but not required.
