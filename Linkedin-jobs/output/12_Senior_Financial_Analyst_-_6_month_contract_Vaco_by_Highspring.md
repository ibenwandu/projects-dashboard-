## Full job description

This is a 6 month contract with permanent potential at the end of the contract




Role: The Senior Financial Analyst is responsible for managing, analyzing, and reconciling intercompany activity across multiple entities within the organization. This role ensures the accuracy, completeness, and compliance of intercompany balances and transactions, supports the consolidation and reporting process, and contributes to improving intercompany workflows and controls.




About the Opportunity

Intercompany Accounting & Reconciliation

Manage the end-to-end process for intercompany transactions, including billing, allocations, eliminations, and settlements.
Prepare and review monthly intercompany reconciliations across multiple legal entities and currencies.
Ensure intercompany balances are properly recorded and eliminated during consolidation.
Partner with local entity accountants to investigate and resolve mismatches and timing differences.
Maintain and improve standardized intercompany procedures, ensuring compliance with corporate policies.




Financial Reporting & Analysis

Support monthly, quarterly, and annual close activities related to intercompany accounting.
Provide analytical insights on intercompany trends, variances, and reconciliations for management reporting.
Assist in preparing consolidated financial statements, schedules, and audit support related to intercompany accounts.
Contribute to foreign exchange analysis and revaluation of intercompany balances.




Process Improvement & Controls

Identify opportunities to streamline and automate intercompany processes and reconciliations.
Participate in ERP and consolidation system enhancements (e.g., SAP, Oracle, NetSuite, Hyperion, OneStream).
Support internal control reviews and ensure adherence to SOX or equivalent compliance frameworks.
Assist in implementing consistent accounting policies across entities to strengthen accuracy and transparency.




Collaboration & Cross-Functional Support

Collaborate with tax, treasury, FP&A, and regional accounting teams to align on intercompany policies and transactions.
Provide training or guidance to junior accountants on intercompany accounting procedures.
Support ad-hoc projects such as reorganizations, mergers, or system integrations affecting intercompany activity.




Qualifications

Education: CPA or working toward designation preferred.
Experience: 4–7 years of progressive accounting or financial analysis experience, including intercompany transactions or consolidation in a multi-entity environment.
Technical Skills:
Strong understanding of IFRS or US GAAP accounting principles
Experience with ERP and consolidation systems (e.g., SAP, Oracle, NetSuite, Hyperion, OneStream)
Advanced Excel and data reconciliation skills




Soft Skills:

Strong analytical and problem-solving abilities
High attention to detail and accuracy
Excellent communication and collaboration skills
Ability to manage multiple priorities in a deadline-driven environment
