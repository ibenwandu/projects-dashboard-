## Full job description

Are you looking to join one of Greater Toronto’s Top 2026 Employers?

The Ontario Medical Association (OMA) advocates for and supports doctors, seeking to strengthen their leadership role in caring for patients. We continually seek to be the trusted voice in transforming Ontario’s health-care system by courageously pursuing best practices, new ideas, solutions, and opportunities to improve.

Job Summary

This position is responsible for enhancing the digital employee experience by ensuring OMA’s devices, applications, and user accounts are secure, reliable, and optimized for productivity. This role takes a customer‑centric, end‑to‑end service approach - proactively improving the quality, stability, and usability of endpoint technologies to support employee satisfaction. By leveraging modern endpoint security practices, data‑driven insights, and continuous service improvement, the position helps create a seamless, friction‑free technology environment that enables employees to work efficiently, securely, engage meaningfully, and achieve their goals.

How You Will Make a Difference


Manage and support corporate endpoint security across Windows, macOS, and mobile devices, ensuring compliance with organizational policies and security standards.
Administer and maintain identity and privileged access controls, supporting secure access for employees and contractors.
Monitor security alerts and incidents, contributing to triage, escalation, and remediation in partnership with senior security teams.
Support endpoint vulnerability remediation and maintain security posture through compliance monitoring and reporting.
Assist in Microsoft 365 security administration, configuration, and monitoring to maintain a secure and compliant environment.
Contribute to security metrics, reporting, and operational insight to inform decision-making and continuous improvement.
Provide operational support, documentation, and participation in change management activities to maintain secure, efficient endpoint services.
