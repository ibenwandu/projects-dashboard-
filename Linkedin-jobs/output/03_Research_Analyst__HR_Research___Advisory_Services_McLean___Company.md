## Full job description

The Research Analyst is part of a team that creates and delivers client-driven, project-based, practical research and advisory services to help HR leaders drive measurable results in their organizations.

Position Purpose

McLean & Company is the fastest growing advisory firm in the world. We’re looking for a Research Analyst to help us achieve our vision of helping leaders shape a workplace where everyone thrives. As a Research Analyst, you’ll work as part of a collaborative team to develop research-informed guides, tools, and templates to help HR clients’ complete key projects such as designing a wellbeing program. Not only will you have exposure to the research you’re working on, you’ll also have the opportunity to give and receive constructive feedback to other project teams with a view to continuously improving the quality of our research. We place a big focus on learning from each other!

Why McLean & Company? McLean is a place where your voice will be heard – a place where you will see the impact of your work with our 30,000+ members. It is a place where you will find community and collaboration, where you will work with diverse individuals with their own unique stories and experiences. McLean is a place where success is celebrated by the whole team – a place where you are encouraged to stretch and grow.

Key Responsibilities

As a Research Analyst, you will:


Participate in development of structured research projects throughout the year leveraging our best practice research processes and methodologies. These research projects, called blueprints, are “Do It Yourself” guides on specific types of client projects that outline a step-by-step process for completing the project.
Conduct primary and secondary research to identify best practices in a subject area, develop frameworks around unstructured topics, and create/integrate memorable insights into practical research blueprints.
Participate in ongoing peer review to give and receive constructive feedback to continuously improve quality of research projects.
Partner with more experienced analysts in advising clients on how best to apply McLean & Company’s practical research solutions in the context of their specific organizational environment and help solve their problems through advisory calls and research requests.
Contribute to a research agenda that meets the needs of members by developing a deep understanding of what McLean & Company members require to be successful HR professionals.
Participate in paid workshops for clients who require more assistance implementing our research.
Participate in critical sales calls to assist the sales staff in pitching the McLean & Company value proposition and in matching our content to the needs of the client.
