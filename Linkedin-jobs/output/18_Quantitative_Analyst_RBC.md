Job Description

WHAT IS THE OPPORTUNITY?

The QIS Quants team develops and supports next-generation investment strategies and is the cornerstone of our Quantitative Investment Strategies (QIS) business. As a front office quantitative analyst, you will leverage RBC’s big data assets to build automated solutions and data analytics models. You will provide analytical support to sales and trading desks across Commodities, FX, Rates, and Equities, and research, design, and implement investment strategies for clients. This role offers a dynamic, collaborative environment to develop innovative solutions that drive trading and risk management activities.

WHAT WILL YOU DO?


Develop and maintain automated data pipelines, infrastructure, signal and index calculation models, and associated processes.
Collaborate with business partners to identify and implement requirements for data quality, integrity, and timeliness.
Ensure governance and controls for all data pipelines, adhering to documentation standards.
Maintain and enhance tools supporting trading desks, quantitative strategies, and risk management.
Develop risk management infrastructure in collaboration with trading desks.
Work with IT and business teams to create data validation methodologies and processes.
Research, design, and implement investment strategies for clients, and build and continually maintain back-testing framework.
Monitor existing strategies and provide day-to-day quantitative support to sales and trading desks.


WHAT DO YOU NEED TO SUCCEED?

Must have:


Bachelor's degree in a quantitative discipline (e.g., Mathematics, Statistics, Engineering, Physics, Computer Science). Master’s degree or higher is preferred, along with at least 2 years of relevant experience.
Demonstrated capability of leveraging relevant technologies (e.g., Python, C++, SQL, q/KDB) to solve problems.
Strong written and oral communication skills, and ability to effectively communicate technical concepts to a non-technical audience; meticulous attention to detail
Knowledge of derivative products, associated valuation methodologies, and trading fundamentals; knowledge of quantitative strategies and back-testing; knowledge of market risk management concepts
Experience in data processing and manipulation.
