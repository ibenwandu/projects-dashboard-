## Full job description

Role Overview

We are looking for a driven and detail-oriented Commodity Business Analyst with a focus on Financial Commodities and Derivatives to join the Capital Markets division. The ideal candidate brings solid banking and finance domain experience, a strong grasp of derivatives products and trade lifecycle, and a proven ability to translate complex business needs into actionable requirements. This is a hybrid role based in Toronto, Ontario, Canada.

Key Responsibilities

Business Analysis & Requirements

Gather, document, and validate business requirements from trading desks, risk management, finance, and operations teams across financial commodities and derivatives.
Produce high-quality BRDs, functional specifications, user stories, process flows, and data mapping documents.
Lead workshops and working sessions with stakeholders to align on scope and solution design.
Conduct gap analysis between current-state and target-state processes, identifying automation and efficiency opportunities.

Financial Commodities & Derivatives

Serve as a subject matter expert on financial commodity instruments including commodity swaps, futures, options, forwards, structured notes, and OTC derivatives.
Analyze end-to-end trade lifecycle: deal capture, valuation, margin management, settlement, reconciliation, and P&L attribution.
Support trading and structuring teams with analysis of pricing models, product configurations, and instrument setups.
Assist in the design and implementation of new derivative products and enhancements to existing product frameworks.

Risk, Compliance & Regulatory

Support compliance with Canadian and international regulatory requirements including ISDA, CSA, OSFI guidelines, and derivatives reporting obligations (e.g., CFTC, EMIR where applicable).
Collaborate with risk teams on market risk, credit risk, and counterparty exposure frameworks specific to commodity derivatives.
Assist in trade reporting initiatives and regulatory change management projects.

Systems & Technology

Work closely with technology and vendor teams on enhancements to commodity trading and risk management platforms (e.g., Openlink Endur, Murex, Calypso, or equivalent).
Define and execute UAT test plans, document defects, and sign off on system changes.
Support data governance initiatives including data lineage, data quality, and reference data management for derivatives instruments.

Stakeholder Engagement & Governance

Act as a trusted liaison between front office (trading), middle office (risk), back office (operations/finance), and IT.
Prepare clear and concise project documentation, status reports, and presentations for senior management.
Participate in Agile/Scrum ceremonies including sprint planning, backlog grooming, and retrospectives.

Required Qualifications

3–6 years of experience as a Business Analyst within banking, capital markets, or financial services.
Solid understanding of financial commodity derivatives — including commodity swaps, futures, options, and structured products.
Demonstrated knowledge of the trade lifecycle from front office to settlement within a banking environment.
Experience working with trading and risk platforms such as Murex, Openlink, Calypso, or similar.
Strong proficiency in requirements documentation: BRDs, use cases, functional specs, and data flow diagrams.
Familiarity with regulatory frameworks: ISDA/CSA documentation, OSFI, and Canadian derivatives regulations.
Excellent analytical, communication, and stakeholder management skills.
Bachelor's degree in Finance, Economics, Computer Science, Engineering, or a related field.

Preferred Qualifications

Experience with commodity derivatives reporting and regulatory change projects.
Knowledge of Agile/Scrum methodologies; CBAP, PMI-PBA, or equivalent certification is an asset.
Exposure to risk frameworks — VaR, PFE, CVA/DVA — within a banking context.
Familiarity with Canadian financial market infrastructure (TMX, CDCC, CDS).
CFA, FRM, or financial derivatives certification (e.g., ISDA CSDM) considered an asset.
Experience with SQL for data analysis and validation.

Core Competencies

Analytical Thinking — Ability to break down complex trading workflows and translate them into structured requirements.
Domain Expertise — Deep understanding of financial commodities and derivatives within a banking environment.
Communication — Excellent written and verbal communication; confident presenting to senior stakeholders.
Collaboration — Strong cross-functional team player comfortable working across trading, risk, IT, and compliance.
Adaptability — Comfortable operating in a fast-paced, change-driven capital markets environment.
