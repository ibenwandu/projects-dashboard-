Job Summary

The Enterprise Data Governance and Quality (EDG) team is part of the Data Innovation Office within Enterprise Data and Analytics organization (EDNA). Our mission is to implement an enterprise model for the governance of data at Equinix and to elevate data as a critical asset. To build trust in data through standardized practices across global programs and lines of businesses, we are seeking a Data Quality Analyst to support the organization in maintaining high‑quality, trusted data across key systems. This role assists with data quality monitoring, issue investigation, documentation, and the execution of data governance processes. The analyst works closely with senior team members, data stewards, and data owners to ensure data quality standards are followed and issues are resolved in a timely manner.

Responsibilities


Create, update, and maintain data quality rules (DQ FRD), metadata, and standards across data domains
Work closely with data owners and stewards to drive corrective actions and remediation while establishing issue tracking, steps to remediate, and root-cause insights
Assist with data cleansing and enrichment tasks across any prioritized data migration initiative
Monitor and report data quality metrics and thresholds
Assist in designing and building effective reporting solutions with business context for accelerating resolution of issues
Participate in process reviews and contribute suggestions to streamline data quality workflows and improve data governance maturity
Support senior analysts in gathering requirements from data stewards and assist in defining measurable DQ metrics and thresholds
Perform data validation, profiling, and documentation activities under guidance.
Validate consistency of data quality rules across Source Systems (SORs)
Assist in managing version control for DQ rules, definitions, and triage protocols.
Maintain all data quality assets within the data catalog
Prepare initial drafts of DQ reports or summaries for review
Willingness to learn and apply data quality best practices through hands‑on work and mentorship
