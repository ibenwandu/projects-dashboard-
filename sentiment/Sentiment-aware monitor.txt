

I want to create a watch list of forex pairs or assets that I have open trades. I want to be able to keep an eye on them so I can be alerted if the market sentiment towards any of them starts to shift. For example, if i have an open short position on USD/CAD and the market starts to shift towards strong USD, the application should do a research to find if there are any fundamentals or news events that is driving the change, if so, it will send me an email notification. The application should allow me to input multiple assets on the watch list as well as the direction of the trades. It will thereafter do a review of the positions every 15 mins and send alerts on any change that is impacting any of the pairs on the watch list. Please give me a plan on how I can achieve this. 


Implementation plan:

	1. What You're Building (High-Level)
A system that:
Maintains a watchlist of FX pairs/assets
Stores trade direction (long / short)
Runs every 15 minutes
Detects sentiment shifts that go against your trade
Identifies drivers (news, macro, fundamentals)
Sends you a clear email alert when risk emerges
Think of it as a macro-aware risk sentinel, not just a price alert.

	2. Core Components (Architecture)
	A. Watchlist & Trade Metadata (Input Layer)
You need a structured way to store:
Field	Example
Asset	USD/CAD
Trade Direction	Short
Bias Expectation	Weak USD
Sensitivity	High / Medium / Low
Notes (optional)	Watching BoC rate decision
Options to store this:
Google Sheet (simple & visual)
Notion database
JSON file
SQLite / Postgres (if you want scalability)
? Recommended to start: Google Sheets (easy + integrates well with n8n)

	B. Market & Sentiment Data Sources
You need three classes of signals:

	1️⃣ Price & Market Behavior (Context)
Purpose: Detect potential sentiment change before fundamentals confirm.
Sources:
OANDA API (since you already use it)
Alpha Vantage (FX prices)
TradingView (webhook or RSS indicators)
Metrics to track:
15m / 1h momentum shift
ATR expansion
Break of VWAP / key levels
Relative strength of USD index (DXY)
⚠️ Important: Price alone does not trigger alerts – it flags investigation.

	2️⃣ News & Macro Fundamentals (Drivers)
This is the heart of your system.
News Sources (APIs or feeds):
ForexFactory calendar (scrape or RSS)
Investing.com news
Financial Juice
AlphaSense (paid)
NewsAPI.org
GDELT (macro/global events)
Macro Data Sources:
FRED (US data)
BoC / Fed statements
CPI, NFP, GDP releases

	3️⃣ Sentiment & Narrative Analysis (LLM Layer)
This is where your AI skills shine.
Use an LLM to answer questions like:
"Is there emerging USD strength narrative in the last 24 hours?"
The LLM will:
Summarize recent news
Detect directional bias
Classify sentiment: Bullish / Neutral / Bearish
Identify root causes

	3. Core Logic Flow (Decision Engine)
	Step-by-Step Logic (Every 15 Minutes)
	Step 1: Load Watchlist
Read all active trades
Loop through each asset

	Step 2: Market Context Check
For each asset:
Is price moving against my trade?
Has volatility increased?
Has relative currency strength changed?
If NO → Skip (no noise)
If YES → Investigate fundamentals

	Step 3: News & Macro Scan
Fetch:
News in last 6–24 hours related to:
Base currency
Quote currency
Broader risk (USD, oil, rates, geopolitics)

	Step 4: LLM Sentiment Analysis
Prompt example:
"Analyze the following news headlines and summaries.
Determine whether market sentiment toward USD has shifted bullish, neutral, or bearish.
Identify any macroeconomic, geopolitical, or monetary policy drivers."
LLM outputs:
Sentiment direction
Confidence level
Key drivers
Whether this conflicts with my trade

	Step 5: Alert Decision
Trigger alert if:
Sentiment conflicts with trade direction AND
Driver is fundamental (rates, inflation, policy, risk-off)

	4. Alerting System (Output Layer)
	Email Notification Structure
Subject:
🚨 Sentiment Shift Alert – USD/CAD Short at Risk
Body:
Asset: USD/CAD
Your Position: Short USD
Detected Shift: USD turning bullish
Drivers:
Hawkish Fed commentary
Strong US PMI surprise
Confidence: High
Time detected: 14:45 EST
Suggested Action:
Monitor closely
Consider reducing exposure

Tools:
Gmail / Outlook SMTP via n8n
Push notifications (Pushover, Telegram, Slack)
