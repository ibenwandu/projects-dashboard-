## Full job description

The Automotive Damage Analyst is responsible for achieving the collision centre production and business goals.

Major areas of responsibility:

· To capture a comprehensive repair plan upfront, thereby minimizing supplements, avoiding delays in production and/or increases in repair costs.

· Support the health and safety of the workforce.

· Support an employee-oriented company culture that emphasizes and appreciates quality, productivity, continuous improvement, goal attainment, employee recruitment, development and retention and high performance.

· Work alongside lead body technicians to prepare a detailed repair plan (blueprint, supplement) for each vehicle as it is dismantled.

· Ability and willingness to follow standardized production processes.

· Ensure that vehicles move through the repair process to meet target dates.

· Take photos of damage as required.

· Convert repair plans to estimating systems and upload them to the insurer.

· Negotiate with insurers a fair and profitable repair.

· Complete understanding of insurance companies’ policies and processes.

· Work with the Parts Department on the ordering and receiving of parts.

· Work with Body Shop Manager, Body & Mechanic Foreman, Paint & Prep Foreman.

Qualifications:

· Minimum 3 years’ experience working as an Automotive Damage Analyst.

· Comprehensive understanding of the entire repair process including body, prep and paint processes.

· Working knowledge of management systems and estimating software: Mitchell and Audatex.

· Auto Body Collision and Damage Repairer Certification (310B). Not a major requirement, but nice to have.

· Ability to communicate and interact effectively with clients and co-workers.
