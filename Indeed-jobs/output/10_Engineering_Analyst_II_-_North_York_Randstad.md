## Full job description

Our client, is further developing an Industry-leading Integrity Management Program which ensures our natural gas system assets are safe and reliable. The Integrity department is accountable for understanding the condition of our assets, confirming they are fit for continued use, or justifying replacement projects if they are nearing end of life.

This role resides in the Facilities Integrity Management Program team. The specific role will have a focus on the creation of Integrity Plans for many critical facilities at Enbridge Gas Inc. The creation of these Integrity Plans requires the collection and review of relevant records including design drawings and past condition monitoring activities, identification of integrity threats applicable to the site, and fitness for service assessments.

Previous experience in station design or station integrity within the oil and gas industry would be beneficial.

Requirements:


Education: the candidates would be required to have at minimum an Engineering degree


Minimum of 4 years or more related work experience

Description:


Coordinate, plan, develop and deliver projects within the Facilities Integrity Management Program (FIMP), which includes assets like pressure vessels, tanks, heat exchangers, pressure relief valves, piping and ancillary components.


Responsible for building and maintaining strong relationships with key internal stakeholders (including Station Operations, Station Engineering, Storage and Transmission, Engineering Construction and Capital Programs) and external stakeholders (including vendors, consultants, and Technical Standards and Safety Authority) and resolving issues and challenges on a program level independently.


Provide support to the facilities integrity assessment program to provide a safe and reliable service that is in compliance with the requirements of applicable standards and regulations in Canada.


Coordinate asset inspections, investigations, assessments, and threat mitigations for station assets.


Support FIMP project prioritization activities within the Asset Plan.


May assist in representing the Company on various industry-related committees and forums including formal codes and standard writing committees or industry task forces.

Technical skills: Experience with MS Office software, Adobe Reader, MS Teams necessary


Helpful but not necessary:

o Experience with ProjectWise or similar records management system

o Experience with Bentley Navigator or View

o Experience with modelling/coding in SPSS software or similar


Hybrid Model (3 days onsite Mon, Tues and Thurs / remote Wed and Fri)

Pay rate is between $30.00 - $32.50 per hour depending on experience.
